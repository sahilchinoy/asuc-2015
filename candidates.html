Q1.3   President

Party

While Dejen captured 17 percent of votes from the opposite party, Student Action, Razavi only won less than 2 percent of votes from respondents who identified with CalSERVE. Overall, Dejen enjoyed a higher degree of party loyalty, receiving 91 percent of CalSERVE’s votes while Razavi secured 73 percent of Student Action’s support.

Supporters of Student Action were more likely to choose Dejen as their second-rank choice for the presidency, unlike members of CalSERVE’s constituency, who preferred CalSERVE, SQUELCH!, BASED. and DAAP’s candidates over Razavi.

Political affiliation
While our results showed that women represented the majority of each party’s constituency, respondents who identified with Student Action were more likely to be male than supporters of any other party. In contrast, 64 percent of respondents who identified as CalSERVE also identified as female.

Q1.4   How did you find out about your first-choice candidate for president?

While Dejen drew most of her support from party affiliation and online campaigning, 51 percent of Razavi’s constituency said they knew him as a personal friend.

Q1.5   Executive Vice President

Party

EVP candidate Lavanya Jawaharlal drew the highest degree of party loyalty of CalSERVE’s executive candidates, receiving 94 percent of support from voters who identified with the party. In comparison, Paul Lee won only 62 percent of Student Action’s support.

Q1.6   How did you find out about your first-choice candidate for EVP?

Jawaharlal and Lee’s constituencies were made up of a similar proportion of friends and acquaintances but Jawaharlal, like her other CalSERVE executive running mates, enjoyed more success online than her Student Action counterpart, with 56 percent of her supporters citing online campaign materials as their first exposure to the candidate.
